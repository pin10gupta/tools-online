What is BMI and How to Calculate

Written by Pintu Gupta | Independent Web Developer & Site Owner

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple screening tool that uses your height and weight to estimate body fat. It helps categorize whether you are underweight, healthy, overweight, or obese. Understanding BMI can help you take control of your health and fitness goals.

What is BMI?

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Healthy weight: BMI 18.5 – 24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25 – 29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

These categories provide a quick way to assess health risks. For example, being overweight increases the risk of diabetes and heart disease, while being underweight can lead to malnutrition and weakened immunity.

How to Calculate BMI

Formula (Metric): BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²)

Formula (US Units): BMI = (Weight (lbs) × 703) ÷ Height (in²)

Example: Weight = 70 kg, Height = 1.75 m → BMI = 22.9 (Healthy weight)

Limitations of BMI

  • Does not distinguish muscle from fat
  • Same chart for men and women
  • Not accurate for children, elderly, or pregnant women
  • Does not show fat distribution

That’s why BMI should be considered alongside other measures like waist circumference and body fat percentage.

Real-Life Example

Alex weighs 85 kg and is 1.70 m tall. His BMI is 29.4, which places him in the overweight category. Priya weighs 55 kg and is 1.65 m tall. Her BMI is 20.2, which is normal. This quick calculation helps both understand their health risks and plan accordingly.

FAQs About BMI

Q: Is BMI accurate for children?
For children, BMI is interpreted differently using age-specific growth charts.

Q: Can BMI predict health risks?
It’s a useful indicator but should be combined with other tests like blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Q: What’s the ideal BMI?
Most adults should aim for 18.5–24.9, but individual goals may vary.

Q: Does BMI differ by gender?
BMI categories are the same for men and women, but body composition differences should be considered.
