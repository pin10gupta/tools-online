Understanding BMI and Why It Matters

Body Mass Index (BMI) is one of the simplest ways to assess health, but many people misunderstand it.

What is BMI?

BMI is a calculation based on weight and height, used as a quick screening tool for health risks.

Categories

  • Underweight: BMI below 18.5
  • Normal: BMI 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25–29.9
  • Obese: BMI 30+

Limitations

BMI doesn’t account for muscle mass, age, or body composition, so it should be used as a guideline rather than a definitive measure.

Why It’s Useful

Despite its limitations, BMI is a valuable indicator for identifying potential health risks quickly.
